One-Day Itineraries

Three ways to spend a day on O'ahu, depending on how far you want to roam from Turtle Bay.

North Shore Day Trip
~25 min loop$40–60 / person
+
  • MorningCoffee close to home, then Waimea Valley for a waterfall hike, then a shrimp truck lunch on the way north.
  • AfternoonSnorkel or tide-pool at Shark's Cove, wander the shops and galleries in Haleiwa town, then watch the surf at Sunset Beach.
  • EveningDinner at Roy's Beach House, right here at the resort.
East Side Adventure
~45 min driveFull day
+
  • MorningLanikai Beach for the calmest water on the island, then the Lanikai Pillbox Trail for the best lookout shot you'll take all trip.
  • MiddayByodo-In Temple tucked into the Ko'olau mountains, then lunch in Kailua town.
  • AfternoonHo'omaluhia Botanical Garden, then a stop at Makapu'u for the lighthouse lookout on your way back.
Waikiki & Honolulu Day
~1 hr driveEarly start recommended
+
  • MorningHike Diamond Head before it gets hot, then a quick visit to 'Iolani Palace.
  • AfternoonBrowse Kaka'ako's murals and shops, then Waikiki Beach for the classic O'ahu postcard moment.
  • EveningSunset catamaran sail, then dinner in Waikiki before the drive back north.

Food & Drink

A short list, on purpose. These are the spots worth the detour.

Shrimp Trucks

Macky's, Giovanni's, and Fumi's all sit along the North Shore — order the garlic shrimp plate.

Haleiwa Town

Walk Kamehameha Highway for shave ice, açai bowls, and local art galleries.

Roy's Beach House

Right on property at Turtle Bay — easiest dinner option after a full day out.
