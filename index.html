Food & Drink on the North Shore

From beachfront bars to farm-to-table lunches and legendary food trucks — a short list, on purpose.

At the resort

Sunset Pool Bar

Right at the pool, with front-row views of the surf break and ocean beyond. Order a Mai Tai, grab some food, and watch surfers from one of the best seats on the North Shore. Open to non-resort guests — live music Thursday & Sunday evenings. Open 10am–8pm, food service 11am–7pm. View menu.

At the resort

Beach House by Roy Yamaguchi

Right on the sand at Kuilima Cove — premium beachfront dining and custom cocktails with your toes practically in the water. Dog-friendly. Open 11:30am–2:30pm and 4:30pm–9pm. View the menu.

Near Turtle Bay · Kahuku

Kahuku Farms

A true farm-to-table experience just minutes from the resort — winner of Newsweek's 2025 Best Farm-to-Table Restaurant in the US. Run by a fourth-generation farming family. Try the acai bowl (made with their own Hawaiian-grown acai), farm pizza, veggie panini, or smoothies. The wagon farm tour is a great add-on. Open Thu–Sun, 11am–4pm. Plan your visit.

Near Turtle Bay · Kahuku Sugar Mill

Shrimp Trucks & Food Trucks at Kahuku

The Kahuku Sugar Mill is a lively cluster of food trucks, restaurants, and shops on Kamehameha Highway. Seven Brothers Burgers is the anchor — known for massive island-style burgers, garlic shrimp, and coconut macadamia shrimp. Also here: Giovanni's and Fumi's for the classic garlic shrimp, Ry's Poke Shack for fresh ahi, Surf N Salsa for Mexican, and Ono Yo for frozen yogurt. Plan for a wander and pick what looks good.

Pupukea · Near Shark's Cove

Pupukea Food Trucks

Pupukea Grill — right across from Shark's Cove — is a North Shore institution. Fresh poke bowls, sushi, kalua pork bowls, wraps, smoothie bowls, and vegan options. The spicy tuna poke bowl is legendary. Da Bald Guy nearby does outstanding plate lunches — kalbi beef and chopped rib eye are the move. Open Tue–Sun. Pupukea Grill menu.

Haleiwa Town

Haleiwa Eats

The historic surf town is packed with great spots. Uncle Bo's Pupu Bar & Grill for local-style seafood and pupus. Waialua Bakery & Juice Bar for sandwiches, house-baked breads, and smoothies. Matsumoto Shave Ice for the classic Hawaiian treat. Surf N Sea for browsing while you snack — leashed dogs welcome.

Scenic drive south · Ka'a'awa

The Crouching Lion

About 30 minutes south down the Windward Coast for modern Hawaiian cuisine, ocean views, and a cold drink. Dog-friendly. Open 11:00am–9pm daily. Learn more.

Beyond The Resort
🦈Shark Cage Diving

View sharks safely from a protective cage just offshore. Book through Hawaii Shark Encounters.

🤿Cage-Free Shark Diving

Swim alongside sharks with no cage, guided by marine biologists, with One Ocean Diving.

🐋Whale & Dolphin Watching

Seasonal humpback whale watching (winter) departs from Haleiwa Harbor aboard the Ho'o Nanea catamaran with North Shore Catamaran Charters.

🎣Deep-Sea Fishing

Charters targeting marlin, mahi-mahi, and ahi depart from Haleiwa Harbor with Go Fishing Hawaii.

🌴Zipline Adventure

O'ahu's longest zipline over a working farm at CLIMB Works Keana Farms in Kahuku.

🌿Waimea Valley

Waterfall, botanical gardens, and Hawaiian cultural sites along an easy paved path. Plan your visit.

🐢Shark's Cove & Laniakea

Snorkel the tide pools at Shark's Cove (summer months) and visit the sleeping sea turtles at Laniakea Beach.

🛍️Haleiwa Town

The historic surf town, a 20-minute drive down the road — boutiques, surf shops, and local restaurants.

One-Day Itineraries

Three ways to spend a day on O'ahu, depending on how far you want to roam from Turtle Bay.

The Perfect One-Day North Shore Adventure
Full dayCustomize at 1:30pm
+
  • 7:00 AMSunrise walk along the Turtle Bay coastline. Grab coffee at Ho'olana Cafe at Turtle Bay.
  • 8:00 AMBreakfast at Sunrise Shack or Ted's Bakery.
  • 9:30 AMExplore Waimea Valley — waterfall and botanical gardens.
  • 11:00 AMRelax on the sand at Waimea Bay.
  • 12:00 PMLunch from a food truck at Shark's Cove or in Haleiwa — shrimp trucks like Giovanni's, or poke trucks like Pupukea Grill, are both great options.
  • 1:30 PMChoose your adventure: snorkel at Shark's Cove, a surf lesson, horseback riding, or ziplining. Our favorite in summer is snorkeling at Shark's Cove.
  • 4:00 PMBrowse Haleiwa Town's surf shops and boutiques, grab shave ice. On the drive back, stop at Laniakea Beach for the sleeping sea turtles and Ehukai Beach Park for the famous Pipeline surf break.
  • SunsetA drink by the pool back at Turtle Bay — the full bar there has great views of the ocean and surfers, plus food options if you're hungry.
  • DinnerBeach House by Roy Yamaguchi, right on the beach at the resort and dog-friendly.
East Side Adventure
~3 hrs total drive timeDepart 8:00 AM
+
  • 8:00 AMDepart Turtle Bay Resort. Grab a coffee and enjoy the scenic drive down O'ahu's Windward Coast.
  • 8:45 AMThe Crouching Lion — breakfast or coffee with dramatic mountain and ocean views. (30–45 min)
  • 9:45 AMKualoa Regional Park — a short beach walk with one of O'ahu's most iconic views, Mokoli'i (Chinaman's Hat). (20–30 min)
  • 10:30 AMByodo-In Temple — located within the Valley of the Temples memorial park, explore the grounds, ring the sacred bell, feed the koi, and enjoy the gardens. There is an entrance fee. (~45 min)
  • 11:15 AMHo'omaluhia Botanical Garden — walking paths and mountain views through one of Hawaii's most beautiful gardens. (~45 min)
  • 12:15 PMLunch in Kailua — Kalapawai Market, Maui Brewing Co., and Buzz's Original Steak House are all great options.
  • 1:30 PMKailua Beach Park — swim, walk the shore, or just relax on one of Hawaii's most beautiful beaches. (1–2 hrs)
  • OptionalSea Life Park — see turtles and dolphins up close. Open 10am–4pm daily except Tuesdays; tickets are recommended online in advance, and there is a parking fee.
  • 5:30 PMLeave Kailua and enjoy the scenic drive back along the Windward Coast.
  • ~7:00 PMArrive back at Turtle Bay. Finish the day with dinner at the resort or sunset from Kuilima Point.

Insider tips: bring swimsuits and towels for Kailua Beach, wear comfortable shoes for the temple and garden, pack water, sunscreen, and a camera, and start early to keep a relaxed pace and avoid traffic. The drive back to Turtle Bay takes about 1.25 hours, whether you return through the island on the H1 or along the coast.

Waikiki & Honolulu Day
Full dayDiamond Head reservation required
+
  • 7:00 AMHike Diamond Head before it gets hot and crowded — a reservation is required in advance for entry and parking. (60–90 min)
  • 8:30 AMBreakfast nearby before heading into town.
  • 10:00 AMKaka'ako — explore the neighborhood's murals, boutiques, and shops.
  • 11:30 AMLunch at Hana Koa Brewing Co. in Kaka'ako.
  • 1:00 PMVisit a museum — the Honolulu Museum of Art or the Bishop Museum are both excellent choices.
  • 3:00 PMHead to Waikiki for a surf lesson or to relax on the beach.
  • 5:00 PMGrab a drink at Duke's Waikiki, or book an afternoon catamaran sail for ocean views.
  • 7:00 PMDinner in Waikiki, or head to Chinatown — Fête is our recommendation.
  • ~8:30 PMDrive back to Turtle Bay — about 1.5 hours, maybe a little less.

Insider tips: book your Diamond Head reservation online well ahead of time — time slots fill up fast — and start early to make the most of the day before Honolulu traffic picks up.

Prefer an ocean-focused day instead? Swap in Hanauma Bay for snorkeling, Sea Life Park, and the Makapu'u Lighthouse trail.
