One-Day Itineraries

Three ways to spend a day on O'ahu, depending on how far you want to roam from Turtle Bay.

The Perfect One-Day North Shore Adventure
Full dayCustomize at 1:30pm
+
  • 7:00 AMSunrise walk along the Turtle Bay coastline. Grab coffee at Ho'olana Cafe at Turtle Bay.
  • 8:00 AMBreakfast at Sunrise Shack or Ted's Bakery.
  • 9:30 AMExplore Waimea Valley — waterfall and botanical gardens.
  • 11:00 AMRelax on the sand at Waimea Bay.
  • 12:00 PMLunch from a North Shore shrimp truck — we like Camaron Shrimp Wagon.
  • 1:30 PMChoose your adventure: snorkel at Shark's Cove, a surf lesson, horseback riding, or ziplining. Our favorite in summer is snorkeling at Shark's Cove.
  • 4:00 PMBrowse Haleiwa Town's surf shops and boutiques, grab shave ice. On the drive back, stop at Laniakea Beach for the sleeping sea turtles and Ehukai Beach Park for the famous Pipeline surf break.
  • SunsetA drink by the pool back at Turtle Bay.
  • DinnerRoy's Beach House, right here at the resort.
East Side Adventure
~3 hrs total drive timeDepart 8:00 AM
+
  • 8:00 AMDepart Turtle Bay Resort. Grab a coffee and enjoy the scenic drive down O'ahu's Windward Coast.
  • 8:45 AMThe Crouching Lion — breakfast or coffee with dramatic mountain and ocean views. (30–45 min)
  • 9:45 AMKualoa Regional Park — a short beach walk with one of O'ahu's most iconic views, Mokoli'i (Chinaman's Hat). (20–30 min)
  • 10:30 AMByodo-In Temple — explore the grounds, ring the sacred bell, feed the koi, and enjoy the gardens. (60–90 min)
  • 12:15 PMHo'omaluhia Botanical Garden — walking paths and mountain views through one of Hawaii's most beautiful gardens. (60–90 min)
  • 2:00 PMLunch in Kailua at one of the town's local restaurants.
  • 3:00 PMKailua Beach Park — swim, walk the shore, or just relax on one of Hawaii's most beautiful beaches. (1–2 hrs)
  • 5:30 PMLeave Kailua and enjoy the scenic drive back along the Windward Coast.
  • ~7:00 PMArrive back at Turtle Bay. Finish the day with dinner at the resort or sunset from Kuilima Point.

Insider tips: bring swimsuits and towels for Kailua Beach, wear comfortable shoes for the temple and garden, pack water, sunscreen, and a camera, and start early to keep a relaxed pace and avoid traffic.

Waikiki & Honolulu Day
~1 hr driveEarly start recommended
+
  • MorningHike Diamond Head before it gets hot, then a quick visit to 'Iolani Palace.
  • AfternoonBrowse Kaka'ako's murals and shops, then Waikiki Beach for the classic O'ahu postcard moment.
  • EveningSunset catamaran sail, then dinner in Waikiki before the drive back north.

Prefer an ocean-focused day instead? Swap in Hanauma Bay for snorkeling, Sea Life Park, and the Makapu'u Lighthouse trail.

On property and beyond

Things To Do

From resort amenities to North Shore adventures a short drive away. Tap any tile for details.

At The Resort
🏊Pools

Four pools on property, including the main resort pool, a quiet adults-only pool, and a kids' pool with waterslides.

⛳Golf

Two championship courses — the Arnold Palmer Course and the George Fazio Course (course availability can vary, so check current status). Book tee times directly through Turtle Bay Golf.

🐎Horseback Riding

Trail rides along oceanfront paths and through ironwood forests, plus pony rides for kids. Book through The Stables at Turtle Bay.

🏄Surf Lessons

Learn to surf right at the resort with the Jamie O'Brien Surf Experience, Turtle Bay's exclusive surf partner.

💆Spa

[ Add spa booking link and signature treatment ]

🎾Tennis & Pickleball

Court rentals and equipment are available through the resort's recreation desk.

🚁Helicopter Tours

Tours depart right from the resort's helipad at Kuilima Point. Inquire with the concierge, or book directly with Blue Hawaiian Helicopters.

🌺Hula, Lei & Ukulele

Daily resort activities rotate through hula lessons, lei-making, and ukulele lessons. See the resort's activities schedule.

Beyond The Resort
🦈Shark Cage Diving

View sharks safely from a protective cage just offshore. Book through Hawaii Shark Encounters.

🤿Cage-Free Shark Diving

Swim alongside sharks with no cage, guided by marine biologists, with One Ocean Diving.

🐋Whale & Dolphin Watching

Seasonal humpback whale watching (winter) departs from Haleiwa Harbor aboard the Ho'o Nanea catamaran with North Shore Catamaran Charters.

🎣Deep-Sea Fishing

Charters targeting marlin, mahi-mahi, and ahi depart from Haleiwa Harbor with Go Fishing Hawaii.

🌴Zipline Adventure

O'ahu's longest zipline over a working farm at CLIMB Works Keana Farms in Kahuku.

🌿Waimea Valley

Waterfall, botanical gardens, and Hawaiian cultural sites along an easy paved path. Plan your visit.

🛍️Haleiwa Town

The historic surf town, a 20-minute drive down the road — boutiques, surf shops, and local restaurants.

From the dog who lives here

Sunny Valentina's Dog Lover Guide

Inspired by Sunny Valentina — the friendly English Cream pup who loves riding shotgun with the windows down and greeting everyone like an old friend. Like Sunny, we hope you explore, slow down, discover new places, and make memories of your own.

Dog-Friendly Walks
  • Turtle Bay coastal paths
  • Pupukea Paumalu Forest Reserve trails (Ehukai Pillbox, Kaunala, and more)
  • Scenic beach pathways around the resort
Dog-Friendly Stops

Uncle Bo's Pupu Bar & Grill

Local-style pupus and seafood, with dog-friendly outdoor seating in Haleiwa.

The Beet Box Cafe

Vegetarian and vegan breakfast, lunch, and fresh juices with pet-welcoming outdoor tables.

Teddy's Bigger Burgers

A quick-casual classic burger spot with outdoor tables for you and Fido.

Matsumoto Shave Ice

The historic shave ice stop with wide open-air bench seating.

Waialua Bakery & Juice Bar

Fresh sandwiches, house-baked breads, and smoothies with cozy outdoor booths.

Camaron Shrimp Wagon

Garlic, coconut, or lemon butter shrimp at spacious dog-friendly picnic tables.

Surf N Sea

A historic boardwalk surf shop where leashed, well-behaved dogs are welcome to browse.

Roy's Beach House & The Crouching Lion

Both also offer dog-friendly outdoor seating — call ahead to confirm.

For a complete map, reviews, and current pet policies, see the BringFido Haleiwa Dog Guide.
